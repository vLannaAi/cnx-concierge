Decorative Symbols

Unicode Symbols for Bullet Points and Sequences

These Unicode symbols are selected for their compatibility with Trirong font and their suitability for professional documents. They provide visual variety for bullet points, sequences, and other common usage in both printed and online documents while maintaining the brand's restrained, institutional character.

Symbol Reference

•

Standard Bullet

U+2022 (•)

The standard bullet point for lists. Most common and universally recognized. Use for standard unordered lists in documents and presentations.

Example:

  • Visa processing services
  • Company formation support
  • Legal structuring assistance
▪

Small Square Bullet

U+25AA (▪)

A solid square bullet for emphasis. Slightly more formal than the standard bullet. Use for secondary lists or when visual distinction is needed.

Example:

  • ▪ Core services
  • ▪ Extended support
  • ▪ Strategic partnerships
▫

White Square Bullet

U+25AB (▫)

An outlined square bullet for subtle lists. Creates visual hierarchy when used alongside solid bullets. Use for nested lists or less prominent items.

Example:

  • ▫ Optional services
  • ▫ Additional support
➣

Rightwards Arrow with Tail

U+21A3 (➣)

Directional arrow with tail indicating progression or navigation. Use for sequences, steps, or directional references. Effective for process flows and call-to-action elements.

Example:

Arrival ➣ Settlement ➣ Business Formation

—

Em Dash

U+2014 (—)

A long dash for emphasis or separation. More formal than hyphens. Use for parenthetical statements, attributions, or creating visual breaks in text.

Example:

Enter CNX — Where Voyagers arrive with intention

…

Ellipsis

U+2026 (…)

Three dots indicating continuation or omission. Use for truncated text, pauses in thought, or indicating additional content. Essential for professional typography.

Example:

Services include visa processing, company formation…

§

Section Sign

U+00A7 (§)

Formal section marker for legal or structured documents. Use for referencing sections, legal citations, or organizing formal content. Maintains institutional tone.

Example:

§ 1. Service Overview | § 2. Terms and Conditions

¶

Paragraph Sign

U+00B6 (¶)

Paragraph marker for editorial or legal documents. Use for referencing specific paragraphs or in editorial contexts. Less common but appropriate for formal documents.

Example:

See paragraph ¶ 3.2 for detailed requirements

◇

White Diamond

U+25C7 (◇)

A white diamond symbol for special emphasis or decorative use. More distinctive than standard bullets. Use sparingly for important items or decorative lists.

Example:

  • ◇ Premium services
  • ◇ Exclusive access
◆

Black Diamond

U+25C6 (◆)

A solid diamond for strong emphasis. More prominent than the white diamond. Use for key points or important callouts. Maintains professional appearance while adding visual interest.

Example:

  • ◆ Critical information
  • ◆ Required actions

Usage Guidelines

Size and Spacing

  • Symbol size should match or slightly exceed text size
  • Standard spacing: 0.5em between symbol and text
  • For lists, maintain consistent indentation (1.5rem)
  • Symbols should align with text baseline

Color Application

  • Primary: CNX Navy (#0c2b5c) for emphasis
  • Standard: Ink (#1a1a1a) for body text lists
  • Secondary: Harbor Gray (#59647a) for less prominent items
  • Accent: Lavender (#76235f) for special callouts (sparingly)

Best Practices

  • Use standard bullet (•) for most lists
  • Reserve special symbols (◇, ◆) for emphasis
  • Maintain consistency within a document
  • Don't mix too many symbol types in one list
  • Ensure symbols are readable at intended size

Technical Implementation

  • Use Unicode entities for web: &#2022; for •
  • Copy-paste symbols directly in word processors
  • Ensure font supports all selected symbols
  • Test symbol rendering across platforms
  • Consider fallback options for older systems
